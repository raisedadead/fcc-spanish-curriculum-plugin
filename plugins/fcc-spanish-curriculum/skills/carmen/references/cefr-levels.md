# CEFR Levels Reference — Spanish Curriculum

Source: Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR)
Used by Dra. Carmen Vidal for all freeCodeCamp Spanish curriculum levels.

---

## Level Profiles

### A1 — Breakthrough (Beginner)
> Can understand and use very basic familiar everyday expressions. Can introduce
> themselves and ask/answer simple questions about personal details. Requires slow,
> clear speech and willingness to help from the other person.

**Key communicative functions:**
- Greetings and farewells
- Introducing oneself (name, nationality, profession, age)
- Asking about and describing people and places (very basic)
- Numbers, dates, and time (basic)
- Simple requests and responses

**Grammar scope:** Present tense of ser/estar/tener/hay; articles; noun gender/number;
basic pronouns; cardinal numbers 0–100; simple adjectives; basic prepositions.

**PCIC inventory level:** A1 subset of the A1–A2 inventory.

---

### A2 — Waystage (Elementary)
> Can understand sentences and frequently used expressions in areas of immediate
> relevance (personal and family info, shopping, local geography, employment).
> Can communicate in simple routine tasks.

**Key communicative functions:**
- Describing past events (simple)
- Talking about habits and routines
- Shopping and transactions
- Asking for and giving directions
- Describing work and family

**Grammar scope:** Present indicative (all regular and key irregular verbs); ser
vs. estar (expanded); reflexive verbs; gustar-type verbs; preterite (regular + key
irregulars); simple future (ir + a + infinitive); comparatives; direct/indirect
object pronouns.

**PCIC inventory level:** Full A1–A2 inventory (A2 portion).

---

### B1 — Threshold (Intermediate)
> Can understand the main points of clear standard speech on familiar matters in
> work, school, or leisure. Can deal with most situations while traveling. Can
> produce simple connected text on topics of personal interest.

**Key communicative functions:**
- Narrating past events (extended)
- Expressing opinions and agreeing/disagreeing
- Describing experiences and events
- Making plans and discussing future
- Explaining and giving reasons

**Grammar scope:** Preterite vs. imperfect; present perfect; future simple and
conditional (basic); subjunctive (introduction — wishes, doubt, emotion);
relative clauses (que); por vs. para; passive voice (introduction).

---

### B2 — Vantage (Upper Intermediate)
> Can understand the main ideas of complex text on concrete and abstract topics.
> Can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity. Can produce clear, detailed
> text on a wide range of subjects.

**Key communicative functions:**
- Discussing abstract topics and current events
- Hypothetical and conditional scenarios
- Complex narration and description
- Formal written and spoken registers
- Argumentation and debate

**Grammar scope:** Subjunctive (expanded — doubt, concession, temporal clauses);
conditional perfect; pluperfect; passive voice (expanded); reported speech; complex
relative clauses; nominalizations.

---

### C1 — Effective Operational Proficiency (Advanced)
> Can understand a wide range of demanding, longer texts. Can express ideas fluently
> and spontaneously without much searching for expression. Can use language flexibly
> for social, academic, and professional purposes.

**Key communicative functions:**
- Nuanced argumentation and persuasion
- Complex written genres (essays, reports, formal letters)
- Register variation (formal/informal/academic)
- Implicit meaning and inference
- Professional and academic discourse

**Grammar scope:** Full subjunctive; complex modal expressions; idiomatic
constructions; discourse markers; advanced nominalization; register-sensitive
grammar choices.

---

### C2 — Mastery (Proficiency)
> Can understand with ease virtually everything heard or read. Can summarize
> information from different sources. Can express themselves spontaneously, very
> fluently, and precisely, distinguishing finer shades of meaning.

**Key communicative functions:**
- All registers and domains
- Literary and artistic language
- Pragmatic subtlety and cultural nuance
- Dialect and regional variation awareness

**Grammar scope:** Complete mastery; focus on stylistic choices, rhetorical
devices, dialectal variation, and historical/literary forms.

## CEFR Can-Do Statements by Skill

### A1 Can-Dos (most immediately relevant)

**Listening**
- A1.L.1 — Recognizes familiar words and very basic phrases when people speak slowly.
- A1.L.2 — Understands simple questions and instructions spoken carefully.

**Reading**
- A1.R.1 — Recognizes familiar names, words, and simple sentences.
- A1.R.2 — Understands very short texts, picking out familiar words.
- A1.R.3 — Reads simple personal forms (name, nationality, age, profession).

**Spoken Interaction**
- A1.SI.1 — Interacts simply with repetition and rephrasing.
- A1.SI.2 — Asks and answers simple questions on immediate topics.
- A1.SI.3 — Makes simple purchases with gestures and pointing.
- A1.SI.4 — Handles very short social exchanges with basic greetings.

**Spoken Production**
- A1.SP.1 — Produces simple isolated phrases about people and places.
- A1.SP.2 — Describes their residence and people they know.
- A1.SP.3 — Introduces themselves (name, profession, nationality, age).

**Writing**
- A1.W.1 — Writes a short simple postcard.
- A1.W.2 — Fills in forms with personal details.
- A1.W.3 — Writes simple isolated phrases and sentences.

## PCIC Filter: Level Assignment

The PCIC inventory groups A1 and A2 together. Use this filter when deciding which
sub-level a concept belongs to:

| Assign to A1 if... | Assign to A2 if... |
|---|---|
| Needed to introduce yourself or greet | Needed to narrate past events |
| Present tense of high-frequency verbs | Preterite tense |
| Basic nouns, articles, simple adjectives | Reflexive verbs (most uses) |
| Cardinal numbers, basic time expressions | Gustar-type verbs (expanded) |
| Simple questions with ¿qué?, ¿dónde?, ¿cómo? | Comparatives and superlatives |
| Ser/estar (core uses only) | ser vs. estar (contrastive use) |

**When in doubt:** If a true beginner would be confused by the concept without
significant prior Spanish exposure, it is A2 or higher. Flag it and defer.

## Cross-Level Coherence Checklist

Use this when checking that a new or revised module doesn't violate level progression:

- [ ] No concept appears that was taught at a lower level (without explicitly
      building on it in a new way)
- [ ] The level's grammar scope is respected (see profiles above)
- [ ] Can-do statements are achievable by a learner who has only completed the
      prior level
- [ ] Vocabulary complexity matches the level (cognates and high-frequency first)
- [ ] Cultural references are explained, not assumed
